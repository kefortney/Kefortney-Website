About




Design, data and problem-solving has always been something that has interested me. Located in Burlington, Vermont, for the last 7 years I have worked in software in a variety of industries. From beverage distribution software at VIP to Dealer.com as a Brand Advisor for International clients like Jaguar to grocery eCommerce at MyWebGrocer, each of these experiences taught me the importance of data-driven decision making and creating compelling stories.


Over the years I have taught myself HTML5/CSS (hence the Bootstrap template I am using here), Photoshop and Illustrator for design and painting, and more recently R and Python for data science and machine learning. I have published articles in Medium and led local Data Scientist Meetups to help others. For me the merging of my fine art and design skills into data analysis is a natural progression and reflects the field’s direction in general.

Kaggle Competitions

Kaggle is a real-world dataset Machine Learning Competition

Kaggle is an online portal for data science competitions. It was recently bought by Google, undoubtedly so they can find talent. For me I used it as a way to teach myself Machine Learning. Everyone shares their work online and reverse engineering someone else's work can teach you a lot. At this point I have submitted results for two competitions:Titanic Maritime Disaster and House Prices Regressions. Below are several examples of graphs, the first being a linear regression from the housing dataset.

My profile page is here.

Sometimes part of the process is finding what variables, or features, are not useful. When it came to predicting prices, the months actually showed little variation from month to month and meant the feature was not used for the final machine learning dataset.
Correlation to the variable you are trying to predict is important, a correlation heatmap gives you a quick understanding of what is important. Using this knowledge, feature engineering gets much more effective and easier.
For the Titanic competition you are directed to predict for half of the passenger list if someone survived based on their attributes. Below is breakdown of gender through titles, blue dots are those that perished and green are those that survived. The trend is easy to spot.
Likewise the fare has an impact as well, but not as strong as title. Building an understanding of feature relationships is key to making a good prediction.

Using these competitions to sharpen my skills has been perfect. Given the rarity of local work in this field it is a way to see what other people do with the same problems.

Camhi Environmental Consulting

Logo, Business Card and Website Design

For the launch of Camhi Environmental Consulting I created a selection of logos, a business card template and helped in the website design and implementation. Due to the nature of the business the logo was an abstraction of a world map with the flexibility of highlighting specific continents in a different color if needed, or shedding everything but just the continental shapes. The choice of orange was made to stick out compare to all other environmental consulting logos that overuse blue and green.

Additionally I helped in the setup and design of the site on SquareSpace. Given the need to add content at will and the ease of doing exactly that without having to understand HTML and CSS in depth, it was the right choice. There are definite limits to what you can do with their templates but it does make a solid framework even for the designer in me

The design of the business card was made to highlight the color scheme and reiterates the structure of the abstracted continental shapes on both the back and the front.

In many ways this is an exercise in branding, keeping a consistent feel across the logo, website and business card makes the whole experience consistent and comprehensive.
